Patient guide

Your health record, finally in one place.

If you've ever tried to gather all your records before a procedure or a new specialist visit, you know how scattered they are. EHI Atlas helps you bring them together — and keep them yours.

If this sounds familiar

Your records live in a dozen different places.

Your primary care doctor uses one portal. The hospital where you had surgery uses another. Your dermatologist is on a third. The lab company that ran your blood work mailed you a PDF. Your insurance company sent statements about claims. Somewhere there's a faxed referral letter from years ago.

"I just need my last A1c, the medication list from my surgeon, and the imaging report from my back MRI — but they're across three portals and one mailed packet, and I'm seeing a new specialist Tuesday."

That's the problem we're solving. Not by inventing a new portal — there are too many already — but by pulling from all of them and giving you one trustworthy view.

How it works

Three steps. You stay in control the whole time.

1CONNECT

Sign in to your hospital portals — once.

We embed Josh Mandel's open Health Skillz connector. You sign in to each portal the same way you do today. Your records download into your browser. Nothing goes to our servers in the clear. Add as many providers as you want.

2BRING TOGETHER

We unify the records — with provenance.

Same condition appearing in two systems? We merge it. Same medication active in one place and discontinued in another? We surface the conflict so you can see both. Lab value buried in a PDF? We extract it into your record. Every fact carries a label showing where it came from.

3USE IT

Ask, share, prepare.

Ask questions about your own record in plain language. Hand off to a new provider in seconds. Prepare for a procedure by handing your surgeon a 30-second briefing. Carry it with you when you travel. You decide what to share, with whom, when.

Privacy

Four promises about your data.

Health information is the most sensitive personal data you have. We treat it that way — and we make the architecture do the work, not just the policy.

1 · Browser-first.

Records live in your browser cache, not on our servers. We never see your data unencrypted. The connector is open source and inspectable.

2 · End-to-end encrypted.

If you choose to share with an AI assistant, your data is encrypted in your browser before it leaves and decrypted only by the recipient. We can't read it in transit.

3 · Built-in redaction.

A redaction studio reviews your record, suggests sensitive items to hide, and saves named profiles you can apply before sharing. Your original records are never modified.

4 · You decide.

What's shared, with whom, and when is your call. We don't sell your data. We don't market on top of it. We don't have a deal with your employer or insurer.

What you can actually do

Once everything's together, the use cases are obvious.

Pre-op briefing
Show your surgeon the five things they need to know before your procedure: active anticoagulants, recent labs, allergies, key conditions, anesthesia notes — in 30 seconds, not 30 minutes of chart-digging.
Second opinion
Share your full unified record with a specialist for a second opinion, with one click and a redaction profile applied. They see the whole picture, not three faxed packets.
New provider
Hand off to a new doctor on day one. Instead of filling out the same intake form for the eighth time, give them your unified record with provenance.
Travel
Carry your record with you. If something happens away from home, the ER has your active conditions, medications, and allergies — with sources they can verify.
Conversational Q&A
"Why am I on this medication?" "When was my last tetanus shot?" "Has my creatinine been trending up?" Ask in plain language; get answers grounded in your real data, with citations to the source.
Conflict resolution
When two systems disagree about whether you're still on a medication, see both records side-by-side and decide what to do — instead of finding out at the next visit.

Frequently asked

FAQ.

Is this HIPAA-protected?
HIPAA covers healthcare providers, payers, and their business associates. As a tool you use directly, EHI Atlas isn't a "covered entity" under HIPAA — but the records you bring in came from HIPAA-protected sources. We treat them with the same standards: end-to-end encrypted, no third-party sale, browser-first storage. The Phase 2 productionization plan includes formal HIPAA-aligned deployment options for clinical use.
What if my hospital isn't connected?
The Health Skillz connector covers 88,000+ providers — most US hospitals on Epic, Oracle Cerner, athenahealth, and others. If yours isn't connected, you can still upload PDFs and CCDA documents you've received directly. The vision-extraction pipeline lifts those into structured form just like the connected sources.
Who can see my data?
By default, only you. When you share — with a clinician, an AI assistant, a new specialist — you choose what's included via a redaction profile, and the share itself is end-to-end encrypted. We don't have a backend that stores your records. We can't read what we don't have.
What does it cost?
Phase 1 is a research demo, free. The eventual product will be free for individuals to connect, view, and ask questions about their own records. Premium features for clinicians and care teams will be paid. We'll never sell your data as a revenue model.
What does the AI know about me?
The AI sees only what you choose to share, in the moment you share it, with end-to-end encryption. It doesn't retain your data between sessions. Every answer it gives cites the specific records it used — so you can verify the source, not just the answer.
What does "provenance" mean in practice?
It means every fact in your unified record knows where it came from. Click a medication, see the original record from your hospital. Click a lab value, see the source PDF page with the value highlighted. Click a condition that came from two systems, see both source records and how we merged them. You're never asked to trust a fact without seeing the source.
Can I delete everything?
Yes, instantly. Records live in your browser cache; clearing them is one click. There's no server-side copy to chase down. If you've shared anything with an AI session, that session is ephemeral by default — closing the tab ends the share.
