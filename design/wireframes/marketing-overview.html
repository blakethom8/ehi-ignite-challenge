The problem

EHI access is solved. EHI usability is not.

Bulk FHIR endpoints, TEFCA, and patient portals have made it possible to get your health data. They have not made it possible to understand it. Records arrive in five different formats, with overlapping facts, conflicting medication lists, and PDFs that no software can read. Clinicians spend half a visit reconstructing history; patients give up.

What we do

The harmonization layer above the connectors.

Connectors get the data out of the EHR. They don't merge it, deconflict it, or make sense of the unstructured pieces. We do.

How we're different

Four things nobody else ships together.

PROVENANCE

Click any fact. See where it came from.

Every harmonized fact carries a FHIR Provenance edge back to the source. Click a medication; see the original record. Click a lab value; the source PDF page opens with the bounding box highlighted. Apple labels per-fact but doesn't merge transparently. We do both.

FIVE SOURCES, ONE RECORD

FHIR + claims + EHI Export + CCDA + PDFs.

Your hospital's FHIR API is one source. Your Medicare claims are another. Your Epic EHI Export TSV is a third. The CCDA from your last referral is a fourth. The lab report PDF is a fifth. We merge all of them — deduplicating, deconflicting, mapping codes — into one canonical patient record.

VISION EXTRACTION

PDFs become structured data.

Drop a lab report on the page. Vision-capable AI lifts the values into FHIR Observations with page-level provenance — the model's confidence, the source attachment, the page and bounding box. Cloud platforms ship document extraction; none connect it to a downstream FHIR record with this kind of lineage.

OPEN AND STANDARDS-NATIVE

FHIR R4 + W3C Provenance, not a proprietary schema.

We don't fork the FHIR schema or invent a vocabulary. Our work uses the published Provenance resource, US Core profiles, CARIN BB for claims, and standard Extensions for what FHIR doesn't cover. Reproducible, inspectable, and citable in the academic literature we extend.

How it works

From scattered records to one clinical view in four steps.

01

Connect your EHRs

Sign in to your hospital portals through the embedded Health Skillz connector. Records cache in your browser, never on our servers.

02

Add your documents

Upload PDFs, scanned faxes, EOBs, and CCDAs you've received from anywhere. Vision AI lifts them into structured form.

03

Review and redact

Browse your unified record. Apply named redaction profiles before sharing — original records are never modified.

04

Use it

30-second clinical briefings for surgeons. Conversational Q&A grounded in your real data. Hand-off packets for new providers.

Open submission · MIT licensed

Built on the published state of the art.

We extend MIMIC-IV-on-FHIR (Bennett et al., 2023), embed Josh Mandel's connector stack with attribution, and adopt the OHDSI vocabulary toolchain. Our contribution is the harmonization layer and the clinician-facing provenance UI nobody else ships. Phase 1 demonstrates the architecture on a worked example; Phase 2 productionizes for multi-vendor coverage.
